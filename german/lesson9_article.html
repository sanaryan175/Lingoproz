Lesson 9: 🧭 Directions & Time in German

Learn how to talk about directions and different times of the day in German.

🗺️ Directions in German

Knowing how to give or understand directions is useful when traveling or navigating a city:

  • Norden – North
  • Süden – South
  • Osten – East
  • Westen – West
  • Richtung – Direction

🕰️ Times of Day

Let’s go over how to say different times of the day in German:

  • Morgen – Morning
  • Mittag – Midday / Noon
  • Nachmittag – Afternoon
  • Abend – Evening
  • Nacht – Night

💡 Grammar & Sentence Usage

  • im = "in the" → used with time and directions. Example: im Morgen = in the morning.
  • Words like nach (to), von (from), and in (in) help you tell location or direction.
  • Die Richtung means "the direction" and is a feminine noun → always use die .

📘 Easy Example Sentences

Ich gehe nach Norden .
I’m going to the north.
👉 nach + direction is used to say "to" a place or direction.

Am Abend esse ich mit meiner Familie.
In the evening, I eat with my family.
👉 am + time of day is used to talk about parts of the day.

Der Supermarkt ist im Westen .
The supermarket is in the west.
👉 im + direction = "in the" + place/direction.

💭 Did You Know?

  • 🕓 Most time words in German use “der” (masculine).
    Example: der Morgen (morning), der Abend (evening).
    👉 But die Nacht (night) is feminine – so it uses “die” .
  • 🧭 The word “Richtung” means direction.
    You’ll see it often in signs: Richtung Berlin = towards Berlin.
  • 🗺️ Compass directions like Norden (north), Süden (south) are all masculine.
    ✅ Use them with words like im (in the), nach (to), and aus (from).
    Example: Ich fahre nach Süden . = I’m going south.
  • 🌄 The word “Morgen” means both morning and tomorrow!
    🕗 am Morgen = in the morning
    👋 bis morgen = see you tomorrow
  • 🎯 Germans greet based on time of day:
    – Guten Morgen = Good morning
    – Guten Abend = Good evening
    – Gute Nacht = Good night
