Lesson 3: Family in German

Learn how to talk about your family and loved ones in German.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Key Family Vocabulary

  • Die Familie – Family
  • Der Vater – Father
  • Die Mutter – Mother
  • Der Bruder – Brother
  • Die Schwester – Sister
  • Die Eltern – Parents
  • Der Sohn – Son
  • Die Tochter – Daughter
  • Die Großeltern – Grandparents
  • Der Großvater (Opa) – Grandfather
  • Die Großmutter (Oma) – Grandmother
  • Der Onkel – Uncle
  • Die Tante – Aunt
  • Der Cousin – Male cousin
  • Die Cousine – Female cousin
  • Das Kind – Child
  • Die Kinder – Children

📚 Grammar Tips

  • Gender & Articles: Masculine → der , Feminine → die , Neutral → das .
  • Plural Form: In German, plurals often change both the article and sometimes the word itself.
    Examples:
    Die Mutter → Die Mütter (the mother → the mothers)
    Der Vater → Die Väter (the father → the fathers)
    🔁 Note: The article for all plurals is die , regardless of gender!
    🔤 Many plurals add dots (Umlauts) like ä , ö , ü or an ending like -n, -e, -er.
  • Possessive Pronouns: "Mein Vater" (my father), "Meine Mutter" (my mother). Gender affects the pronoun ending.
  • Verb Agreement: “Meine Eltern sind nett” (My parents are nice).

💬 Usage in Context – Talking About Family

Ich habe einen Bruder und eine Schwester.
I have a brother and a sister.
🧠 Note: “Bruder” is masculine → einen, “Schwester” is feminine → eine

Mein Vater ist Arzt.
My father is a doctor.
🩺 “Arzt” means doctor (male). If female: “Ärztin”

Meine Großeltern wohnen in Berlin.
My grandparents live in Berlin.
🏙️ “Großeltern” = grandparents (plural of “Großvater” & “Großmutter” )

Wie heißt deine Mutter?
What is your mother’s name?
❓ “Wie heißt” ...? is a common way to ask someone’s name in German.

Meine Familie ist sehr groß.
My family is very big.
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 “groß” = big/large → talking about number of family members.

🧠 Fun Facts

  • In German culture, family titles are often used with respect — e.g., Mutti for mom or Vati for dad affectionately.
  • Plural endings change depending on gender and root word — e.g., Bruder → Brüder , Schwester → Schwestern .
  • The word Familie is feminine, so we say die Familie .
