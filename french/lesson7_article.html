Lesson 7: My Home in French

Learn how to describe your home, rooms, furniture, and where things are!

🏡 Vocabulary: Rooms in the House

  • la maison – the house
  • l’appartement – the apartment
  • le salon – the living room
  • la chambre – the bedroom
  • la cuisine – the kitchen
  • la salle de bain – the bathroom
  • le couloir – the hallway
  • le jardin – the garden
  • le balcon – the balcony

🛋️ Vocabulary: Furniture & Items

  • la table – the table
  • la chaise – the chair
  • le lit – the bed
  • la lampe – the lamp
  • l’armoire – the wardrobe
  • la porte – the door
  • la fenêtre – the window
  • le canapé / le sofa – the couch / sofa

🧭 Prepositions: Where Things Are

  • sur – on
  • sous – under
  • à côté de – next to
  • derrière – behind
  • devant – in front of
  • entre – between
  • dans – in

Example: La lampe est sur la table. (The lamp is on the table.)

🛠️ Sentence Structure – Made Easy!

French sentence structure is usually: Subject + Verb + Rest. Let’s look at some examples:

  • La maison est grande.
    (The house is big.)
    La maison = the house, est = is, grande = big (feminine form)
  • La cuisine est moderne.
    (The kitchen is modern.)
    La cuisine = the kitchen, moderne = modern
  • La fenêtre est ouverte.
    (The window is open.)
    La fenêtre = the window, ouverte = open
  • La salle de bain est propre.
    (The bathroom is clean.)
    La salle de bain = the bathroom, propre = clean
  • La chambre a un grand lit.
    (The bedroom has a big bed.)
    La chambre = the bedroom, a = has, un grand lit = a big bed

📘 Usage in Context – Easy French Sentences

J’habite dans une petite maison.
(I live in a small house.)
J’ = I, habite = live, dans une petite maison = in a small house

Mon appartement a deux chambres.
(My apartment has two bedrooms.)
Mon appartement = my apartment, a = has, deux chambres = two bedrooms

La lampe est sur la table.
(The lamp is on the table.)
La lampe = the lamp, est = is, sur la table = on the table

Le jardin est beau et vert.
(The garden is beautiful and green.)
Le jardin = the garden, est = is, beau et vert = beautiful and green

💡 Did You Know?

  • 🪟 Many French homes have volets (shutters) on windows for privacy and temperature control.
  • 🚪 French nouns have genders: la maison (feminine), le salon (masculine).
  • 🧼 It’s common in France to greet with a kiss on the cheek when you visit someone’s home.
  • 🌿 Eco-friendly design is popular in France—solar panels, proper insulation, and waste sorting are encouraged.
  • 🛋️ Adjectives must agree with the gender and number: une grande maison (a big house), un joli salon (a pretty living room).
  • 🛏️ The word chambre (room) is used in many home words: chambre d’amis (guest room), chambre d’enfant (children’s room).
  • 📚 Spring cleaning is also popular in France—called nettoyage de printemps.
